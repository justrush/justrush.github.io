## Case Studies

### Case Study: AI Call Capture & Booking Recovery
**The Business:** A residential plumbing company with a six-person field team, fielding a heavy mix of routine service calls and after-hours emergencies.

**The Challenge:** Every plumber was under a sink, in a crawlspace, or on an emergency call for most of the day. When the phone rang, it either went unanswered or someone stopped mid-job to take it. And in plumbing, a missed call isn't just a lost appointment — it's a burst pipe or a water heater emergency walking straight to whichever competitor answers first.

**What I Built:** An AI-powered call and chat handling layer, deployed directly into the business's existing phone line and field service software — answering every call, capturing the right diagnostic details, booking straightforward jobs automatically, and triaging true emergencies to a live person immediately. No new number to give out, no new app for customers to learn.

**The Impact:** The business now answers 100% of inbound calls, around the clock — closing the exact gap that costs trades businesses hundreds to thousands of dollars per missed call. Technicians stay heads-down on the work that actually generates revenue, instead of splitting focus between a wrench and a ringing phone.

### Case Study: Estimate-to-Cash Follow-Up & Review Capture
**The Business:** A commercial and residential painting company generating a steady volume of project estimates every week, most of them multi-thousand-dollar jobs.

**The Challenge:** Estimates went out, and then largely fended for themselves. Nobody had time to circle back on a quote three days later, let alone three weeks later — so warm leads went cold, and jobs that should have closed simply disappeared. Separately, the business was doing great work on-site but capturing Google reviews for only about 20-25% of completed jobs, which meant most of that goodwill never showed up where future customers were actually looking.

**What I Built:** A structured, fully automated 8-touch follow-up sequence running over 21 days from the moment an estimate goes out — combined with a review capture campaign built around two specific customer segments (recent reviewers on other platforms, and past customers with no review on file), running quietly in the background through the business's existing tools.

**The Result:** Estimates now get consistent, professional follow-up without anyone lifting a finger, and the review capture strategy was built to move the business from roughly 20-25% capture up toward a 35-45% target — turning finished jobs into the online reputation that wins the next bid.

### Case Study: Operations Intelligence Dashboard
**The Business:** An excavation and site-prep contractor tracking two years of job data across their field service platform, but with no consolidated view of where the money actually was.

**The Challenge:** Revenue looked healthy on paper. Job volume was up year over year. But nobody could say with confidence whether the business was actually more profitable — or just busier — especially with equipment and fuel costs eating into margin in ways that weren't obvious job-by-job.

**What I Built:** A full data pipeline pulling job exports directly from the company's field service software into a structured benchmark workbook, with a custom job classifier organizing every job by type and a year-over-year analysis layered on top.

**The Result:** The dashboard surfaced something the top-line numbers were hiding: revenue had been flat year over year despite real growth in job volume, because average ticket size was quietly declining. That single insight reframed the business's entire pricing and bid-strategy conversation — a problem that was invisible until the data was structured correctly.

### Case Study: Dynamic Performance & Compensation Dashboard
**The Business:** A residential HVAC and air conditioning company with a five-person field team, anchored by technicians at very different stages of skill and experience — from a standout Lead Technician to newer hires still building their craft.

**The Challenge:** A flat, one-size-fits-all bonus structure wasn't cutting it. The business needed a way to reward its best performer properly without demotivating the rest of the team, but "performance" meant something different for a seasoned Lead Tech than it did for a technician two years into the trade. A static spreadsheet bonus plan couldn't account for that — and it also couldn't flex with the business's actual financial performance month to month.

**What I Built:** A dynamic performance and compensation dashboard that pulls directly from the business's financials and each employee's live performance data — job completion rates, callback rates, revenue generated, customer satisfaction scores — and cross-references it against each technician's individual skill level and experience tier. Instead of one bonus formula for everyone, the system calculates a compensation structure tailored to each employee, strategically incentivizing the specific skills and behaviors that actually move the needle for someone at their stage — junior techs get incentivized differently than senior techs, and the whole structure moves with the business's real numbers rather than a fixed target set once a year.

**The Result:** A compensation model that's a genuine win-win — the business only pays out more when the business is actually performing better, and each employee has a clear, individualized, and achievable path to earning more based on what they're actually good at and where they're growing. The dashboard has since been extended beyond compensation: it now automatically builds individualized professional development and training plans for each technician, and doubles as the foundation for the business's formal performance review process — turning what used to be a once-a-year guessing exercise into a living, data-backed conversation about where each person stands and where they're headed.

### Case Study: Competitive & Margin Intelligence
**The Business:** An electrical contracting company operating in a growing metro market seeing early signs of private equity consolidation among competitors.

**The Challenge:** The ownership team could feel the market shifting — new competitors, buyout activity — but had no structured way to understand the scale of the threat, or whether their own pricing was even capturing the margin it should.

**What I Built:** A competitive threat briefing mapping active PE roll-up activity and projected new-entrant risk in the local market, paired with a full pricebook margin analysis across 130+ line items — flagging cost-integrity issues hiding inside the numbers.

**The Result:** The analysis found a mean margin around 71% across the pricebook, with specific line items flagged for correction — giving ownership a clear, binary strategic choice instead of a vague sense that "something's changing" in the market.
